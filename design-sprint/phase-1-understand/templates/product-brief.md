## About This Activity

The Product Brief is the essential starting point for any sprint. It aligns the team on what product or service is being designed, who it is for, and what problem it needs to solve — before any research, sketching, or solutioning begins. Without a shared brief, the sprint risks solving the wrong problem or designing for the wrong user.

| Without a Product Brief | With a Product Brief |
|---|---|
| Team members start with different assumptions | Everyone is aligned on the same context |
| Interviews ask inconsistent questions | Interviews are focused on the right users and problems |
| HMW notes diverge in direction | HMW notes converge on a shared problem space |
| Phase 2 Problem Statement is hard to agree on | Problem Statement is grounded in shared evidence |

**Example:**
Product: *"An internal tool for HR teams at mid-sized companies"*
→ Brief: *"Target user: HR manager (5–200 employee company). Core problem: Too much time spent manually chasing onboarding paperwork, leading to delayed start dates and poor new-hire experience. Constraint: Must work within existing Slack-based workflows. North Star: A new hire completes all onboarding paperwork before Day 1 without any manual follow-up from HR."*

## How DesignSprintAgent Runs This Activity

The agent executes the following sequence in Copilot Chat. At each step it will:

1. **Prompt** — present a facilitating question or instruction to the group
2. **Collect** — ask participants to share their input directly in chat

## Step 4 — Define Constraints and North Star Goal

**What the agent does:** Prompts the group to surface known constraints (technical, business, time, resource) and to articulate the North Star — the ideal end state for the user if the sprint succeeds.

> *"Two final inputs: (1) What constraints does the team need to work within? (technical limits, business rules, time, integrations) and (2) What does success look like for the user at the end of this sprint — what should they be able to do or feel that they can't today?*

| Downstream Activity / Artefact | How this activity informs it |

| **User Journey Map** | Target users and the problem context define whose journey to map and which pain points to prioritise |
